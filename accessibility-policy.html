Accessibility Policy

Our commitment to an inclusive and accessible website experience for all users

Welcome to our online website accessibility policy. Our website has been designed to be as accessible as reasonably possible and to meet the needs of our disabled users. It is important to us that the widest possible audience is able to view our website. It is also a legal requirement under the Equality Act 2010.

Our Commitment to Accessibility

We recognise that different forms of disability affect access to websites. By making this website accessible we are adhering to standards that ensure all users can access this site. We have also approached the site design to make it easy to navigate and use for all users.

We also ensure new content meets accessibility requirements and we continually review existing content to maintain the highest possible standards of usability.

What You Can Do on This Website

All users should be able to:

Navigate the site and use all online functions, easily find contact details, and complete all of these tasks using common forms of assistive technology, including screen readers, keyboard-only navigation, and voice control software.

Adjusting Text Size

If you wish to increase or decrease the size of our text, you can do this using your internet browser. For example:

Google Chrome: Menu > Zoom > Increase % (the default is 100%)

Most modern browsers offer similar zoom and text-size controls, either through the browser menu or keyboard shortcuts (typically Ctrl/Cmd + or −).

Contact Us About Accessibility

If you have any questions regarding the accessibility of this site or experience any difficulty using the website, please contact us with details of your query and we will do our best to assist you promptly.
